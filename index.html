NLP Practitioner Field Guide

Strategy Elicitation

A beginner's step-by-step checklist for extracting how someone produces a specific result — so you can replicate or teach it.

What is a strategy? The precise internal sequence (pictures, sounds, feelings, self-talk) a person runs to produce a specific outcome. Elicit it cleanly and you can model it, install it, or coach around a broken link in the chain.
1

Set the Context

prime the unconscious
  • Pick one specific, repeatable outcome (e.g. "how you decide to buy a book," not "how you make decisions")
  • Ask them to recall a specific past instance when they did it well
  • Have them close their eyes and step back into it fully — see, hear, feel what they did
2

Find the Trigger

locate step one
  • Ask: "What was the very first thing that happened?"
  • Listen for modality: did they see something, hear something, or feel something first?
  • Note it using VAK shorthand (see below). This is your starting link.
3

Walk the Sequence

capture the syntax
  • Ask: "Then what happened? And then what?" — stay curious, stay quiet
  • For each step, clarify: internal or external? pictures, sounds, self-talk, or body sensation?
  • Write the sequence in order using the notation below

V — Visual

Ve = external (seeing it)
Vi = internal (picturing it)

A — Auditory

Ae = heard aloud
Ad = self-talk (digital)

K — Kinesthetic

Ke = physical sensation
Ki = emotion / gut feel

4

Find the Decision Point

locate the test
  • Ask: "How did you know you were done? How did you know it was right?"
  • This is the exit criterion — usually a feeling, a click, or an internal "yes"
  • Note the submodalities: was it bright? loud? located somewhere in the body?
5

Test & Refine

verify the model
  • Play the sequence back to them out loud. Ask: "Did I miss a step?"
  • Have them run it on a new instance and confirm it produces the same result
  • If the result breaks, a step is missing or out of order — re-elicit that link only
