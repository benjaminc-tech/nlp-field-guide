Eliciting Your Own Strategies

A step-by-step protocol for extracting the exact sequence behind a past success — so you can run it on demand.

Before you start — set up the conditions. Self-elicitation fails when you stay in analytical mode. Engineer the environment first.
Voice memo open, ready to record Eyes closed, speaking out loud Pick one specific past instance Plan to do the full protocol 3× (three instances)
1

Anchor a Specific Instance

bypass the sanitized version
  • Pick one outcome you've produced well more than once (e.g. "the morning I wrote the Drift Method PDF in one sitting")
  • Name the date, place, and what you were wearing — these are re-entry hooks
  • Close your eyes. Step back in. See what you saw, hear what you heard, feel what you felt
Trap: "I usually do X" is your conscious mind summarizing. You want the actual instance, present tense, first person.
2

Narrate Out Loud — Don't Write Yet

bypass compression
  • Start recording. Narrate as if describing it to someone who wasn't there
  • Use present tense: "I'm sitting at the desk. I open the laptop. I see..."
  • Include the mundane — what you did with your hands, what you looked at first, what you said under your breath
  • Resist editing. If you catch yourself explaining why, stop and return to what you did
3

Transcribe & Map the Syntax

see the structure
  • Transcribe the recording (or have Claude do it). Now you can see it from outside
  • Next to each step, tag the modality using the notation below
  • Look for the actual starting trigger — often earlier than you think

V — Visual

Ve = external (seeing it)
Vi = internal (picturing it)

A — Auditory

Ae = heard aloud
Ad = self-talk (digital)

K — Kinesthetic

Ke = physical sensation
Ki = emotion / gut feel

4

Interrogate the Obvious

find the missing links
  • For each step, ask: "What happened just before this that I skipped?"
  • Ask: "How did I know to do this step and not something else?"
  • Identify the exit criterion: "How did I know I was done?" (this is usually a Ki)
  • Use a second Claude session as your elicitor — paste your transcript, let it ask questions
5

Triangulate & Test

pattern vs. story
  • Repeat steps 1–4 on two more instances of the same outcome
  • Compare all three. The common sequence is your strategy — the differences are noise
  • Write the distilled strategy as a short protocol: trigger → steps → exit
  • Run it deliberately on a new instance. If the outcome holds, it's modeled. If not, a link is missing or out of order
